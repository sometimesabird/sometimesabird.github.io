    • Do Social Media Ads Matter for Political Behavior? A Field Experiment

    With Mateusz Stalinski (Chicago)

    We exploit Facebook's introduction of a filter hiding ads from the feed as a unique opportunity to study the effects of ads on political behavior. In a pre-registered experiment, we randomly assigned participants to one of the two conditions – hide political ads (treatment) or hide alcohol ads (control) – for several weeks preceding the 2020 US elections. We first verified that the intervention induced exogenous variation in exposure to political ads. While the overall modest effect on turnout was insignificant, this masked important heterogeneity. Though ads lead Democrats to be slightly more motivated to vote, Republicans become substantially less motivated. We explore reasons for this effect, such as natural variation in ad content: the majority of Facebook ads on users’ feeds skewed Democratic. Lastly, the effect on measures of affective polarization and informedness was negligible.
